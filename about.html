About Me

Hello! I'm Alen John. I'm passionate about cybersecurity and ethical hacking. I specialize in monitoring security incidents, detecting threats, and conducting penetration tests.

I have hands-on experience with various tools such as Wireshark, Metasploit, and Nmap, and have participated in several security assessments and vulnerability analyses.

I’m constantly learning and evolving to stay ahead in the ever-changing cybersecurity landscape. My aim is to help organizations stay secure by identifying weaknesses and providing solutions.

My interest in ethical hacking started early when I became fascinated by how systems work and how vulnerabilities can be exploited. Since then, I have dedicated my time to ethical hacking, focusing on responsible security practices.

In my free time, I enjoy participating in Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges, experimenting with security tools, and writing about cybersecurity techniques.

My goal is to become a top cybersecurity professional, helping organizations secure their infrastructure. I am also working towards earning my CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker) certification.

I strongly believe that cybersecurity is not just about hacking—it's about making the digital world a safer place for everyone.
